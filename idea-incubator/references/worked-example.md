# Worked examples & templates

Two examples: a fast **Sprint loop** (the default mode) and the full **Deep
canvas**. Use them to calibrate your own questions and to show the user what
"done" looks like.

---

## Sprint loop example (fast path) — a meal-prep service idea

```
IDEA: A weekly meal-prep delivery for busy parents.
CUSTOMER: Dual-income parents of young kids in my zip code.
OUTCOME: Dinner handled all week without thinking about it.

ASSUMPTIONS (ranked by mortality):
  1. Parents will pay $X/week rather than just order takeout.   ← leap of faith
  2. I can cook+deliver 20 orders/week solo.
  3. Repeat orders, not one-and-done.

RISKIEST ASSUMPTION: They'll pay weekly vs. defaulting to takeout.
THE TEST: Pre-sell one week to 10 parents from my network at full price,
          collect a $20 deposit. No cooking until deposits are in. (Concierge MVP.)
DECIDES IT: persevere if ≥4 of 10 put money down; pivot if ≤1.
DO THIS WEEK: DM/ask 10 parents and request the deposit by Friday.
```

Note what the Sprint did NOT do: no logo, no website, no menu R&D, no LLC. The
only question that matters is "will they pay," so the test attacks exactly that
and nothing else. That's the bias for action.

After running it, the founder returns in **Re-entry mode** and the result gets
logged (see the loop log template at the end of this file).

## Deep canvas example — the music industry (the canonical template)

Use this to show the user what a full work-up looks like, or to calibrate your
own questions. It's the example that shaped this skill.

```
IDEA: Make a living as a working songwriter.
DOMAIN: Music industry        DATE: —

HERO: The songwriter (you).
PAIN: Has songs and talent but no repeatable path to income from them.
VALUE PROP: Songs that move people and fit commercial structures publishers,
            artists, and sync buyers can actually use.
WHY YOU / WHY NOW: Volume + taste + willingness to play the game.

GOAL (the win): Money via performance & mechanical royalties, licensing/sync
            deals, and/or an artist deal (records / merch / royalties).

FRAMEWORK (unit of work): A ~3:30 song that fits one of 3 proven structures,
            with a quality bar high enough to survive a live room.

WORKFLOW (idea → goal):
  1. Write 100 songs            [gate: did you actually finish 100?]
  2. Play shows & refine        [gate: which songs land live?]   [tools: gigs]
  3. Record the best 10–12      [gate: are these your A-material?] [tools: studio]
  4. Network                    [tools: relationships, scenes]
  5. Showcase to publishers/labels [gate: any real interest/cuts?]
  6. Repeat as long as you can afford it or until you hit the goal.

VIABILITY READ:
  Demand:         Real but brutally competitive; sync market is growing.
  Feasibility:    High — cost is mostly time and persistence.
  Economics:      Long tail; a few cuts/syncs can compound. Slow to start.
  Differentiation:Your voice + catalog depth; hard to copy a great catalog.
  Timing/Distro:  Distribution (the rooms, the relationships) is the bottleneck.
  Founder fit:    The whole game is whether you can sustain the volume.

PATHS:
  Failure:     You don't finish your first 100 songs.
  Dream:       1–2 decent cuts, a few licensing deals, a decent living playing
               shows on your own terms.
  Exceptional: 360 deal with a major, ~$75k/yr from music, working alongside
               famous and talented people.
  Unicorn 🦄:  #1 song/record, multiple albums, lifelong career — only if you're
               willing to play at the highest level.

POINT OF VIABILITY: First paid cut or sync + repeatable show income.
KILL CRITERIA:      You stop finishing songs / can no longer afford to continue.
GOAL EXIT:          A decent living on your own terms (the Dream path).

RISKIEST ASSUMPTION: That you'll sustain the writing volume long enough to get lucky.
CHEAPEST NEXT EXPERIMENT: Finish + test 10 songs in front of live rooms this quarter.

DO THIS NEXT:
  □ Commit to the first 100-song batch with a deadline.
  □ Book a recurring gig to refine in public.
  □ Identify 5 sync/publisher targets and what they actually license.
```

### Why this maps to any domain
- **Hero** = the customer (or you, if you're the audience).
- **Framework / unit of work** = the song → a feature, a post, a client project.
- **Workflow** = write 100 → ship 100 small things and learn.
- **Gates** = the "did you finish?" checkpoints that catch most failures.
- **Paths** = always name failure / dream / exceptional / unicorn so the user
  chooses upside *and* accepts the most-likely-failure honestly.

## Loop log template

Create `idea-log-<slug>.md` and append one block per loop:

```
# Idea log — <idea name>

## Loop 1 — 2026-06-28
Assumption tested: Parents will pay weekly vs. defaulting to takeout.
Test run:          Pre-sold one week to 10 network parents, $20 deposit.
Pre-committed line: persevere if ≥4 of 10 pay; pivot if ≤1.
Result:            3 of 10 paid; 2 more said "next month."
Verdict:           PERSEVERE — demand is real but price/segment needs a look.
Next assumption:   Can I deliver 20 orders/week solo without burning out?
Next action (by 2026-07-05): Cook+deliver the 3 paid orders by hand; time everything.
```

On re-entry, read the latest block first and pick up exactly there.
